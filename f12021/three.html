2021 Drivers and Where are they now

( Red Bull )

Max Verstappen

After winning his first championship, he had won four in a row and is looking to take his fifth in a row with his Red Bull team for the 2025 season (63 career wins)

Sergio Perez

Stayed in Red Bull until 2024, currently not on the grid (10 career wins).

( Mercedes )

Lewis Hamilton

Has only won two races since Saudi Arabai 2021. Has yet to claim his eighth world championship. (105 career wins)

Valtteri Bottas

Moved to Alfa Romeo in 2022 until 2024 and is now a Mercedes reserve driver (10 career wins).

( Ferrari )

Charles Leclerc

Still in Ferrari, was the runner-up for the 2022 season (8 career wins).

Carlos Sainz

Stayed in Ferrari until 2024, and is now a Williams driver (4 career wins).

( McLaren )

Lando Norris

Still in McLaren, was the runner-up for the 2024 season (5 career wins).

Daniel Ricciardo

Stayed in McLaren until 2022, replaced a AlphaTauri driver halfway thourgh the 2023 season, until he was replaced by another driver halfway through the 2024 season (8 career wins).

( AlphaTauri )

Pierre Gasly

Stayed in AlphaTauri until 2022, and is now a Alpine driver (1 career win).

Yuki Tsunoda

Stayed in AlphaTauri until 2025, and two races into the 2025 season, he has been promoted to Red Bull.

( Alpine )

Fernando Alonso

Stayed in Alpine until 2022, and is now a Aston Martin driver (32 career wins).

Esteban Ocon

Stayed in Alpine until 2024, and is currently a Haas driver (1 career win).

( Aston Martin )

Sebastian Vettel

Stayed in Aston Martin until he retired at the end of the 2022 season (53 wins).

Lance Stroll

Still in Aston Martin.

( Williams )

George Russell

Moved to Mercedes in 2022 (3 career wins).

Nicholas Latifi

Stayed in Williams until 2022, currently not on the grid.

( Alfa Romeo )

Kimi Raikkonen

Retired at the end of the 2021 season (21 career wins).

Antonio Giovannci

Stayed in AlfaRomeo until 2021, currently not on the grid.

( Hass )

Mick Schumacher

Stayed in Haas until 2022, currently not on the grid.

Nikita Mazepin

Stayed in Haas until 2021, currently not on the grid.
